        The Possible<\/h2>\n\n\n

        We must first establish the proper meaning of \u201cpossible\u201d. I think many people, including many in science, do not grasp the meaning of this concept. Even though it is an important concept in science and all other spheres of thought.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Here is the definition of possible that I am using in this article:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        \n

        Relating to a claim for which there is some evidence for. A claim is possible if some evidence exists to support it being true and there is none that proves that it is not true.<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n\n\n

        A claim is possible if you can find some evidence to support it. <\/h3>\n\n\n

        The claim does not have to be proven true, there simply needs to be some evidence in favor of it. I consider a theory which is proven possible as it meets the above criteria. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

        In order to take any claim seriously, you should first establish that the claim is at least possible. There must be some amount of evidence that supports the claim. There must be a reason that the claim is potentially true. That the claim being made matches the fact of reality. If you cannot do this, then your claim is arbitrary. There is no way to connect it back to reality of any kind.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Something is not \u201cpossible\u201d simply because one cannot think why it is not true. One has to have a reason we should consider it to be true.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        \u201cX is possible\u201d means: <\/p>\n\n\n\n

        \n

        That according to the context of my knowledge, there is some evidence that X is true and none that proves that it is not true.<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n\n\n\n

        Note that there may not be very much<\/em> evidence that supports X, but there must be at least some evidence.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        The evidence might not be very conclusive, and the truth of X may still need to be verified. Tt least I have established the possibility<\/em> of the claim. At least I have established some reason to believe that the claim might be valid.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        Let\u2019s take an example.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

        I wonder if humans might have evolved via a process of natural selection from some other species. I have not yet established that this is possible. It is just speculation. That I have guessed that this might be the case is not the same as establishing that it is possible. A guess in the absence of evidence does not establish that something is possible.<\/p>\n\n\n
